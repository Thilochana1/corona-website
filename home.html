General vaccination, assuming May after medical care (Japan) 1/20

Regarding the vaccination of the new coronavirus, it was found that the Japanese government expects to start vaccination of the general public around May, following priority vaccination of medical staff, elderly people, and people with underlying diseases.

New corona vaccination, more than 220,000 people in 2 days (India) 1/19

The Indian government announced on the 17th that 224,301 people have received the new coronavirus vaccine. Due to its scale, attention is focused on India's vaccination program, which is called the "world's largest vaccine program."

Norwegian authorities "does not affect vaccination plans" (Norway) 1/19

Norwegian drug officials announced on the 19th that more than 20 elderly people died within 6 days of being vaccinated against the new coronavirus, "there is no impact on the vaccination plan." In Norway, 23 people, including the elderly with serious underlying illness, have been reported to have died within 6 days of being vaccinated with Pfizer.

"low-income countries have only been vaccinated 25 times" 1/19

At the beginning of the WHO Board of Directors, which began on the 18th, Secretary-General Tedros touched on the status of vaccine supply and pointed out that developed countries and others have offered high prices to pharmaceutical companies and are prioritizing the securing of vaccines. According to Tedros, 49 countries, including developed countries, have been vaccinated about 39 million times, but only one low-income country has received only 25 vaccines.

Vaccination reduces infection rate by 30-60%? (Israel) 1/15

The health maintenance organization "maccabi" reports that the infection rate decreased by 60% 14 days after vaccination. The vaccine is said to lead to immunity after two inoculations. This time, it is only a survey after the first vaccination, and further research is needed to measure the effect with high accuracy.

Vaccination, finally 10 million(USA) 1/14

The US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) announced on the 13th that about 29.4 million doses of the new coronavirus vaccine have already been distributed, of which about 10.3 million doses have been inoculated.
